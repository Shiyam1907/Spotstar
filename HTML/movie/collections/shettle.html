Krejcikova Bags Maiden
Wimbledon Title

                                       Barbora Krejcikova, the 31st seed from Czechia, made history by winning her maiden Wimbledon singles title in a thrilling three-set final against Italy's Jasmine Paolini. The 6-2, 2-6, 6-4 victory marked Krejcikova's second Grand Slam triumph after her 2021 French Open triumph. The 27-year-old became only the fifth Czech woman to win the Wimbledon singles title in the Open Era, joining the likes of Martina Navratilova, Jana Novotna, Petra Kvitova, and Marketa Vondrousova. Krejcikova's emotional journey was further highlighted by her close connection with the late Jana Novotna, who had inspired her to turn professional and win a Grand Slam title. Despite facing a determined Paolini and a pro-Italian crowd, Krejcikova showcased her resilience and mental toughness to clinch the title and secure her return to the top 10 of the WTA rankings.
