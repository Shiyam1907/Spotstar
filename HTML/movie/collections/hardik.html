                                       In the final match of the 2024 ICC Men's T20 World Cup, held on June 29 at the Kensington Oval in Barbados, India defeated South Africa by seven runs to claim their second T20 World Cup title. India batted first, scoring 176 runs for 7 wickets in their 20 overs, with notable contributions from players like Rohit Sharma. In response, South Africa managed to score 169 runs for 8 wickets, falling short despite a valiant effort. This victory marked a significant achievement for India, equaling the record for the most T20 World Cup titles alongside England and the West Indies. The match showcased high-stakes cricket, with India's bowlers holding their nerve in a tense finish, ultimately securing a memorable win for the team and its fans.
