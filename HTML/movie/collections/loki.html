Loki

                                       "Loki" is a critically acclaimed American television series created by Michael Waldron, based on the Marvel Comics character of the same name, and is part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). Premiering on Disney+ on June 9, 2021, the series follows Loki, portrayed by Tom Hiddleston, after he escapes with the Tesseract during the events of "Avengers: Endgame," creating an alternate timeline. Captured by the Time Variance Authority (TVA), Loki is forced to help track down a dangerous variant of himself, leading to a journey that explores themes of identity, free will, and redemption. The show features a strong supporting cast, including Owen Wilson and Gugu Mbatha-Raw, and has been praised for its innovative storytelling, visual style, and character development. Following the success of its first season, a second season was announced, further expanding the narrative and its implications within the MCU.
