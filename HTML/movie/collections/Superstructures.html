Superstructures

                                       "Superstructures: Engineering Marvels" is a National Geographic series that explores some of the world's most impressive and innovative architectural feats, showcasing the engineering techniques and technologies that make these structures possible. The series delves into iconic buildings and infrastructures, such as the Singapore National Stadium, which features a record-breaking dome made from over 11,000 tons of steel, highlighting the challenges faced by architects and engineers in their construction. Each episode combines stunning visuals with expert insights, revealing the intricate designs and materials used to create these monumental works while emphasizing their significance in modern architecture and society. Through this series, viewers gain a deeper appreciation for the creativity and ingenuity involved in building superstructures that push the boundaries of design and engineering.
