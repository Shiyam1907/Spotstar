99 Problems

"99 Problems" is a seminal hip-hop track by legendary rapper Jay-Z, released in 2004 as the lead single from his album "The Black Album." The song features a hard-hitting, rock-influenced production by Rick Rubin and showcases Jay-Z's lyrical prowess, as he tackles topics ranging from dealing with rap critics and radio politics to a confrontation with the police. The catchy hook, "I got 99 problems but a bitch ain't one," has become an iconic part of hip-hop culture, with the song's social commentary and confrontational lyrics cementing its status as a classic. "99 Problems" is widely regarded as one of Jay-Z's signature songs and a defining moment in his illustrious career, demonstrating his ability to blend storytelling, social awareness, and undeniable musical talent".
