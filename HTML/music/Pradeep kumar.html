Pradeep kumar

"Pradeep Kumar was a renowned Indian actor who made significant contributions to Hindi, Bengali, and English-language films during his prolific career. Born as Sital Batabyal in 1925, he began his acting journey in Bengali cinema before transitioning to Bollywood, where he gained widespread recognition for his lead roles in iconic films like "Anarkali," "Nagin," and "Raj Hath." Known for his versatility, Kumar collaborated with several legendary actresses, including Madhubala, Meena Kumari, and Mala Sinha, and was praised for his captivating performances and on-screen charisma. Over the course of his five-decade career, he won the prestigious Kalakar Award for Lifetime Achievement in 1999, cementing his status as one of the most influential and respected actors in the history of Indian cinema".
